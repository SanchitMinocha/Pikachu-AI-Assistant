### RAT 3.0 – Reservoir Assessment Tool
**Status:** Active | **Impact:** NASA-featured | **Type:** Open-source research tool

RAT 3.0 is arguably Sanchit Minocha's most impactful and well-known work. It is a scalable, robust, efficient, and user-friendly software architecture for satellite-based reservoir monitoring. The tool uses satellite imagery (Landsat, Sentinel) to estimate reservoir water surface area, storage capacity, and inflow/outflow.

**Who uses it:**
- NASA USAID SERVIR SEA (Southeast Asia regional program)
- Mekong River Commission
- Bangladesh Water Development Board
- Other regional water management agencies

**Why it matters:**
In many developing nations, in-situ monitoring infrastructure is limited or non-existent. RAT 3.0 provides a free, satellite-based alternative that governments and agencies can use to monitor reservoirs critical for irrigation, drinking water, and flood control.

**Sanchit's role:** Primary developer. He delivered in-person training workshops to agency staff for real-world implementation.

**Technologies:** Python, Satellite Imagery (Landsat-8, Sentinel-1/2), Google Earth Engine, Hydrological modeling

### GRILSS Dataset – Global Reservoir sILt and Sediment Survey
**Status:** Published | **Type:** Research dataset | **Significance:** First-of-its-kind globally

GRILSS is the first-of-its-kind global reservoir sedimentation dataset. It was curated by Sanchit using satellite observations and ML to quantify how much sediment has accumulated in reservoirs worldwide, and how much storage capacity has been lost.

**Why it matters:**
Reservoir sedimentation reduces storage capacity over time, threatening water security globally. Without data on sedimentation rates, it's impossible to plan effectively. GRILSS fills a critical gap in global water resource data.

**Technologies:** Python, Google Earth Engine, Machine Learning, Satellite Data

### RECLAIM – Reservoir Capacity Loss Prediction Framework
**Status:** Deployed as web app | **GitHub:** https://github.com/SanchitMinocha/RECLAIM-Web-Application

RECLAIM is a globally scalable machine learning framework to predict reservoir sedimentation and capacity loss using satellite observations. It was deployed as a web application on Apache Server using Django, making it accessible to researchers and practitioners worldwide.

**Why it matters:**
RECLAIM automates the prediction of future reservoir capacity loss, giving water managers and policymakers a tool to plan ahead before critical water storage is permanently lost.

**Technologies:** Python, Django, Apache Server, Machine Learning, Satellite Data, HTML/CSS

**Live deployment:** Apache Server-based web app

### Experimental Multi-Sensor Reservoir Area Estimation
**GitHub:** https://github.com/SanchitMinocha/Experimental-Multi-sensor-Reservoir-Area-Estimation | ⭐ 2

Water area estimation using multi-sensor fusion: Landsat-8, Sentinel-1 (SAR), and Sentinel-2 (optical) satellite imagery processed via Google Earth Engine. The multi-sensor approach provides robustness against cloud cover and improves accuracy.

**Technologies:** Google Earth Engine, Landsat-8, Sentinel-1, Sentinel-2, Python, Jupyter Notebook
